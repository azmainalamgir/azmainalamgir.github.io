About Me

I was originally born in Bangladesh but spent most of my formative years in the state of Mississippi, home of the blues and land of the best fried catfish known to man.

Growing up in the Deep South, I’ve always found myself questioning how things worked. Why is the sky blue? How did the universe come to be? When will Arsenal (my favorite football club) win the league again? Although teachers and textbooks did an adequate job of answering some of these questions on a superficial level, I still yearned for a deeper understanding for things that were unknown to me. A pivotal moment came during high school when I began doing research at a local university near my hometown. By putting myself in the shoes of a scientist, I discovered how new knowledge can be uncovered through a formalized process consisting of experimentation and observation. This experience was precisely what I was looking for to quell my inquisitive tendencies and sparked my initial desire to pursue a career in research.

After high school, I attended Northwestern University for my undergraduate studies, where I majored in Chemical Engineering and was fortunate to gain my first full-blown research experience in the lab of Dr. Milan Mrksich. As part of the Mrksich lab, I worked on designing a label-free platform for the high-throughput analysis of small molecules, which subsequently became the subject of my Honors Thesis. Outside of class, I served on the Executive Board of the Northwestern chapter of American Institute of Chemical Engineers and as a tutor (funded by the Federal Work Study Program) for several classes.

My academic and research activities eventually brought me to Cornell University, where I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering. My goal at Cornell is to acquire the necessary skills so that I may fulfill my long-held desire of becoming an independent researcher. I will be periodically updating this website as my research projects progress, so be sure to check back later to see how I’d doing!

When I’m not in the lab, you can catch me at the gym pumping iron, in my bed napping, on the fields playing soccer, or on my couch binging Netflix.
