Research Activites

Over the years, I've been involved in a number of research projects in disciplines ranging from chemistry, materials science, and engineering. Listed below is a timeline of my reserach activities, along with brief descriptions for each project (please refer to CV for full list of publications and presentations).

Sept. 2016-June 2019

Mrksich Lab


Northwestern University

Description: In the fall of 2016, I joined Dr. Milan Mrksich’s lab after developing a newfound interest in organic chemistry from my undergraduate coursework. I partnered with a graduate student to help develop a label-free photochemical platform for rapidly analyzing small molecules using SAMDI-MS (Self-Assembled Monolayers Matrix Assisted Laser Desorption/Ionization – Mass Spectroscopy), a tool developed by the Mrksich group for analyzing surface reactions and capturing analytes in solution through the use of functionalized alkanethiolates on gold surfaces. During the period that I worked (including two consecutive summers at Northwestern), I was able to demonstrate the non-selectivity of a diazirine photochemical probe for the immobilization of various small molecules as well as apply this photo-immobilization technique to characterize the activity of a cytochrome p450 enzyme and to monitor a Suzuki-Miyaura chemical coupling reaction.

Publication: Traceless Immobilization of Analytes for High-Throughput Experiments with SAMDI Mass Spectrometry

June 2016-Aug. 2016

Ray Lab


Jackson State University

Description: Funded by an NSF PREM Fellowship, I worked as an undergraduate research assistant in the lab of Dr. Paresh at Jackson State University during the summer of 2016. The goal of the project I collaborated on involved the synthesis of hydrochar and biochar (highly functionalized carbon-based materials) from cellulose and the characterization of those materials using UV-Vis, Fluorescence, and Raman spectroscopy. This work was later expanded to water purification studies and ultimately led to a publication in a peer-reviewed journal.

Publication: Multifunctional Biochar for Highly Efficient Capture, Identification, and Removal of Toxic Metals and Superbugs from Water Samples

2014-2015

Leszczynski Lab


Jackson State University

Description: As a high school research assistant, I worked at Jackson State University in Dr. Jerzy Leszczynski’s lab, where I was involved in a project related to the synthesis and application of anion-binding receptors. During this period, I was specifically tasked with conducting spectroscopic studies via UV-Vis and Fluorescence spectroscopy as well as performing theoretical binding studies using computational tools to quantitatively determine binding affinity of the synthesized receptors with environmentally relevant anions. This work was presented at regional scientific conferences and local science fairs.
